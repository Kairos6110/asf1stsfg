Operation Oxide

Launch from FOB Bandit at 1900, land on the designated LZ, hit the communications sites holding civilians, capture HVT LIMO if present, and hand network control back to Golden Eagle through on-site control-room access.

Briefing Location
FOB Bandit / South Cape Station
Step-Off
1900 hours / designated LZ insertion
Primary Target
HVT LIMO, enemy commander observed at BRAVO
Network Task
Access control rooms and link with Golden Eagle

Situation

Enemy forces are coercing civilians across multiple comms locations and using the network infrastructure to sustain control. Earlier recon placed commander LIMO at BRAVO, but the target may have displaced to another node.

This is a large-scale civilian rescue mission. Target ID discipline is mandatory before every engagement.

Execution

Clear each site, protect civilians, seize control-room access, and report link status to Golden Eagle once the network is secured.

  • Immediate ActionRapidly neutralize hostile forces actively controlling or threatening civilians.
  • Secondary ActionCapture HVT LIMO if found and restore friendly network control from the local control rooms.
  • Comms LinkAuthenticate with Golden Eagle and confirm the data stream is operational before moving on.

Civilian Density

Each objective reports 6-14+ civilians on site. Expect crowded interiors and crossfire risk around control buildings and walkways.

Threat Pattern

Automated turrets, mortars, rooftop or catwalk snipers, and heavy gunners are confirmed at several locations. Generators are present at ALPHA, BRAVO, and ECHO.

Radio Drill

Use the scripted Oxide to Golden Eagle authentication exchange once a control-room terminal is secured and the link is ready.
