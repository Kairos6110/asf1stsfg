Operation Jaguar

Fireteam Jaguar will strike across the Seal Islands to stop Sentinel’s illicit missile and NV-optics transfer to Al-Hayat, kill the off-site anti-air commander LOCKSMITH, seize his Portable Authentication Module, exploit the battery’s comms network, and destroy the outbound weapons package before it leaves Auroa.

Brief
South Cape Station, 2000 hours
Insertion
2011 departure, land southeast of target, then use local watercraft to reach the island IP
Cargo
23 Javelin missiles and multiple NV optics delivered to Emelius Port for probable transfer to Al-Hayat
Finish
Destroy the target package, retain acquired OPINFO, and RTB when the chain is complete

Situation

ISR confirmed that an LCU delivered 23 Javelin missiles and multiple NV optics to Emelius Port. Sources assess that the cargo is intended for Al-Hayat, the Middle Eastern terror group currently operating against US allies in Darb Khandaq. Sentinel is now directly tied to the transfer chain.

Before the weapons package can be demolished, the team must exploit a time-sensitive target of opportunity. SIGINT fixed the location of the anti-air commander LOCKSMITH, currently holding an off-site meeting on Entry Island. His Portable Authentication Module is required to decrypt data in the Anti-Aircraft Battery comms building and expose Sentinel’s offshore clients and operational picture.

Execution

  • Phase 1 Land southeast of the target area, move by watercraft to the forested IP, and recon the HVT meeting site before the strike.
  • Phase 2 Kill LOCKSMITH, seize the PAM, then pivot by watercraft to the Anti-Aircraft Battery for prisoner recovery and comms exploitation.
  • Phase 3 Continue on foot to the listening-post objective and the dockside weapons package, then destroy the shipment and RTB.

Primary Risk

If the weapons package moves, Sentinel succeeds in shipping anti-armor missiles and night-fighting gear to an active terror client outside Auroa. The demolition window is the decisive point.
